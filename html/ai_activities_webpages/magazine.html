Magazine Cut-outs

In this activity, learners will create artwork, upload it to edit with AI, print, and iterate various times. Learners will create a simple black and white image, and upload it to an AI to edit one or two things from it. They will print it and then layer magazine cutouts on their drawing, take a picture, and edit it again. They will then print this one as well. They will then showcase the four artworks to visualize their process.

Activity Instructions

Teacher Learner

1. Create simple network:

Use the art materials to draw something you like, only in black and white. Keep it simple.

2. Edit with AI:

Upload your artwork and edit one or two things with the help of AI. Print the output.

3. Edit with magazines:

Use magazines to cutout pieces to layer on top of your drawing. Take your time to add details to reimagine your drawing.

4. Edit with AI:

Display your four artworks side by side over a roll of brown paper and connect them like a flowchart, explaining what changed with each iteration. share your process with the group.

5. Exhibit and share:

Display your four artworks side by side over a roll of brown paper and connect them like a flowchart, explaining what changed with each iteration. share your process with the group.

6. Reflect:

Use the discussion prompts to guide the conversation about ethics focusing on the ethical principles of transparency

Guiding Questions for AI Ethics Literacy

Based on OECD AI principles.

Inclusive growth, sustainable development, and well-being
  • Enhancing Creativity: Between adding magazine cutouts by versus having the AI edit your drawing, which process felt more creative?
Respect for the rule of law, human rights and democratic values, including fairness and privacy
  • Supporting human agency and autonomy: In which steps did you have the most control over the final image? Who is the final "author" of the last image?
Transparency and explainability
  • Telling how AI outcomes are generated: Look at the jump from your collage (Image 3) to the final AI-edited version (Image 4). Can you explain precisely how the AI made its changes? What specific element in your collage did it "see" to generate a specific part of the new image? How did your prediction of the AI outcome compare to the actual output? If the result was a surprise, does that make the tool more creative or just more random?
Robustness, security, and safety
  • Ensuring robustness: How well did the AI handle your collage? Did it produce a coherent image, or did it generate strange errors or nonsensical artifacts? Did the tool operate in the way you expected?
  • Foreseeable Misuse: Could this tool be used in harmful ways?
