Homage to a Local Artist

In this activity, learners will create artwork based on Tania Sívertsen’s work by describing it to image-generative AI. Learners will print three iterations, frame them, and include the prompts they used to get to that result. They will frame their results alongside Tania’s artwork to make the prompted process visual and tangible as an exhibit. Through making these comparisons and by stimulating the conversation with prompts, learners will engage with OECD AI ethics principles based on their personal design experiences with AI.

Activity Instructions

Teacher Learner

1. Look at artwork

Get familiar with the original artwork by an artist, Tania Sívertsen.

2. Describe the artwork to AI:

Try to replicate her artwork using text-to-image AI generative software, focusing on what descriptions most accurately represent the style.

3. Print: Compare and contrast

4. Repeat Step 1 through 3 until you have three homages printed out.

5. Exhibit and share:

Display the original and your artworks side by side, then share them with the rest of the group, and explain how you achieved the result.

6. Reflect:

Use the discussion prompts to guide the conversation about ethics focusing on the ethical principles of Human-centered values and fairness and Transparency and explainability.

Guiding Questions for AI Ethics Literacy

Based on OECD AI principles.

Respect for the rule of law, human rights and democratic values, including fairness and privacy
  • Ensuring fairness: Who is the author of the final artworks you created? Who should get the credit—you for writing the prompts, the AI for generating the image, the developers who built the AI, or the original artist who provided the inspiration?
  • Human Agency and Autonomy: Compared to the process of recreating by hand, in which moments did you feel most in control and creative and when did it feel like the AI was making the decisions for you?
  • Bias and Representation: Did the AI introduce any elements or styles you didn't ask for?
Transparency and Explainability
  • Understanding the AI system: When you received an image from the AI, could you tell exactly why it looked the way it did based on the words you used? If you changed one word in your prompt and the image changed a lot, was the reason for the change obvious?
  • The "Black Box": Did this activity make you feel like you understand how the AI works on the inside, or did it just help you get better at giving it instructions to get the result you want? What is the difference between these two things?
  • Explaining the outcome: How could you explain to Tania how your image was created? Does the AI give you an explenation?
