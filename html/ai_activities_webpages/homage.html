Homage to a Local Artist

In this activity, you will create your own art based on Tania Sívertsen’s work by describing it to AI. You will create three iterations, frame them, and include the prompts you used to get to them. Then, display your results alongside Tania’s original artwork to make the prompted process visual and tangible as an exhibit. By comparing the artwork and talking about the differences, you will engage with ethics principles. You can do this alone, with a partner, or in a small group.

Activity Instructions

1. Take a look at the artwork:

Get familiar with the original piece by artist Tania Sívertsen.

2. Describe the artwork to AI:

Try to replicate her artwork using text-to-image AI generative software, focusing on what descriptions most accurately represent the style.

3. Save outputs

Download and, if possible, print the artworks.

4. Repeat Step 1-3 until you have three homages printed out.

Until you have three homages downloaded or printed.

5. Exhibit and share:

Display the original and your designs side by side, then share them with the rest of the group, and explain how you achieved the result.

6. Reflect:

Use the discussion prompts to guide the conversation about ethics.

AI Ethics conversation prompts

Fairness and agency
  • Fairness: Who is the author of the designs? Who should get the credit—you for writing the prompts, the AI for generating the image, the developers who built the AI, or the original artist who provided the inspiration?
  • Human Agency and Autonomy: Compared to the process of recreating by hand, in which moments did you feel most in control and creative and when did it feel like the AI was making the decisions for you?
  • Agency: Did you feel in control compared to drawing things by hand? Did it feel like the AI was making the decisions for you?
  • Bias: Did the AI introduce any elements or styles you didn't ask for?
Transparency and Explainability
  • Understanding the AI system: Could you relate the words in your prompt to the image output? Did it make sense?
  • Transparency: Did this activity make you feel like you understand how AI works, or did it help you get better at giving it instructions to get the result you want? What is the difference between these two?
  • Explaining outcomes: How could you explain to Tania how your image was created? Does the AI give you an explanation?
