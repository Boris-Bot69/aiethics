Prototyping and Pitching AI

In this activity, learners will design an AI product to aid their school reach Sustainable Development Goals, and use text-generative AI to create a pitch, and image-generative AI to illustrate its use case. The product creators will take turns sharing their work to their peers, who will roleplay as stakeholders to evaluate the ethical principles in play with the products.

What's Needed

Also use other materials you can think of.

  • Comic/Manga panel examples
  • Image-generative AI
  • Text-generative AI
  • A4 frame (1 per group)
  • Printer

Guiding Questions for AI Ethics Literacy

Based on OECD AI principles.

Inclusive growth, sustainable development, and well-being
  • Sustainable Development Goals: How does your AI product contribute to achieving the Sustainable Development Goals at your school?
Respect for the rule of law, human rights and democratic values, including fairness and privacy
  • Stakeholder perspectives: How did different stakeholders (learners, teachers, parents, principal) evaluate the ethical issues at stake with your AI product?
Transparency and explainability
  • Explaining your product: Can you clearly explain how your AI product works and what data it uses to make decisions?

Activity Instructions

1

Design

Design an AI product that will help your school reach Sustainable Development Goals. Consider what ethical features are important in your AI product, based on the OECD principles.

2

Illustrate and pitch

Use image-generative AI to illustrate your AI product, and text-generative AI to create a pitch that you will present to the rest of the groups.

3

Share

Depending on number of groups, either two groups share to each other or groups share one by one to the whole group.

4

Feedback

Swap designs with another group and comment on their product from four selected perspectives: learners, teachers, parents, principal, school staff, investor (pick 4) and in relation to OECD AI principles.

5

Iterate

Redesign based on comments with a focus on AI Ethics.

6

Share

Small groups share with the whole group: (1) What you designed with AI ethics features, (2) what changed based on feedback, (3) what you would do next, and (4) what you learned.

7

Reflect

Use the discussion prompts to guide the conversation about ethics, focusing on the ethical principles.
