Prototyping and pitching AI

In this activity, learners will design an AI product to aid their school reach Sustainable Development Goals, and use text-generative AI to create a pitch, and image-genertive AI to illustrate itâ€™s usecase. The product creators will take turns sharing their work to their peers, who will roleplay as stakeholders to evaluate the ethical principles in play with the products.

Activity Instructions

Teacher Learner

1. Design:

Design an AI product that will help your school reach Sustainable Development Goals. Consider what ethical features are important in your AI product, based on the OECD principles.

2. Illustrate and pitch:

Use image-generative AI to illustrate your AI product, and text-generative AI to create a pitch that you will present to the rest of the groups.

3. Share:

Depending on number of groups, either two groups share to each other or groups share one by one to the whole group.

4. Feedback:

Swap designs with another group and comment on their product from four selected perspectives: learners, teachers, parents, principal, school staff, investor (pick 4) and in relation to OECD AI principles. What ethical issues are at stake?

5. Iterate:

Redesign based on comments with a focus on AI Ethics.

6. Share:

Small groups share with the whole group with a focus: (1) What did you design with AI ethics features in mind (say it again), (2) what changed based on the feedback from your peers (focus on AI Ethics), (3) what would you do next with your AI product?, and (4), what did you learn?

7. Reflect:

Use the discussion prompts to guide the conversation about ethics, focusing on the ethical principles of ____ and _____.
