Expanded frames

In this activity, learners will create artwork and expand its frame with AI and with craft materials from an initial A8 to A4 size.

Activity Instructions

1. Create an artwork:

Create an original artwork using the materials available on an A8 size paper. It could be anything; donâ€™t limit yourself! Once you are done, take a picture of it.

2. Expand the frame with AI:

Now, another group member will use this picture and expand it with image-generative AI. The expansion should be an A7 size.

3. Expand with materials:

A third group member should now expand it using materials to an A6 size.

4. Repeat steps 2 and 3 until you reach A4 size

5. Roles:

Depending on the group size, you can all start with one A8 art and then the person to your right or left can start the cycle.

6. Share:

Present your finalized A4 artwork and share the different frame iterations an art exhibition that details the progress of the original artwork to the expanded ones and the imitated ones to showcase the process of the artwork being generated.

7. Reflect:

Use the discussion prompts to guide the conversation about ethics, focusing on the ethical principles of ____ and _____.

Materials

  • A8 - A4 printable sizes
  • Paint and brushes
  • Markers
  • Color pencils
  • Texture materials

Guiding Questions for AI Ethics Literacy

Inclusive growth, sustainable development, and well-being
  • Enhancing creativity: Comparing the human and the AI expansions, which process felt more creative? Which process resulted in more interesting or surprising outcomes?
  • Augmenting human capabilities: In this activity, you and the AI were partners. Did the AIâ€™s contributions feel like they augmented your creative vision, or did they redirect it in ways you didn't intend? Did the AI's expansion inspire new ideas for your own turn with the craft materials?
Respect for the rule of law, human rights and democratic values, including fairness and privacy
  • Supporting human agency and autonomy: Who is the final author of the A4 artwork? How much of the final piece was guided by human decisions versus AI decisions?
  • At what point, if any, did it stop feeling like your artwork?
  • Respecting privacy and data protection: Is the AI good or not at expanding your style, and what does this say about its training data?
Transparency and explainability
  • Telling how AI outcomes are generated: When the AI expanded the frame, could you explain why it added what it did? For example, if it added a forest next to a house, was it because of a specific color, shape, or texture in your drawing, or was it a random choice?
  • Fostering understanding: Did this activity make you feel like you were collaborating with a partner that "understood" your art, or simply using a tool that followed a set of untransparent rules?
Robustness, security, and safety
  • Ensuring robustness: The AI had to interpret both your initial clean drawing and the photos of your later, physically-crafted additions (which might have texture, glue, etc.). How well did the AI handle these different types of input? Did it ever produce glitches, errors, or nonsensical expansions?
Accountability
  • What was your responsibility as a user in this activity? If the AI generated something you thought was low-quality, biased, or strange, did you feel a responsibility to "fix" it with your additions, or to stop using it?
  • What responsibility do the creators of the AI tool have for the images it generates? What features could they have built into the tool to ensure accountability?
